## 2. The Equivalence Principle

We define the magnitude of subjective reality using the following equivalence equation:

$$
\mathcal{E} = \mathfrak{m} \cdot \mathbb{C}^2
$$

Where:

* **$\mathcal{E}$ (Experiential Energy):** The magnitude of subjective reality; the raw power of "being" and radiance.
* **$\mathfrak{m}$ (Mental Mass):** The inertia of the psyche. This represents entropy, ego-structure, trauma, and rigid definitions of self that resist cognitive acceleration.
* **$\mathbb{C}$ (Coherence):** The speed of internal information integration, analogous to the speed of light.

This relationship implies that as Coherence ($\mathbb{C}$) increases, a small liberation of Mental Mass ($\mathfrak{m}$) results in an exponential increase in Experiential Energy. Conversely, to approach the maximum speed of processing (the "Singularity" of experience), $\mathfrak{m}$ must approach zero.

## 3. The Fourier Transformation of Consciousness

To understand the mechanism of this conversion, we map the cognitive state to signal processing domains. We posit two distinct modes of operation for the conscious substrate:

### 3.1 The Space-Time Brain ($f(t)$)
This is the "Particle" state, operating in the linear **Time Domain**. Experience is sequential, discrete, and separable. It is characterized by high Mental Mass and perception of distinct objects and identities.

### 3.2 The Frequency Brain ($F(\omega)$)
This is the "Wave" state, operating in the **Frequency Domain**. Experience is simultaneous, resonant, and interconnected. It is characterized by zero mass and high spectral density.

### 3.3 The Transformation
The transition between these states is described by the Fourier Transform:

$$
F(\omega) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t) e^{-i\omega t} dt
$$

This integral represents the summation of the linear timeline ($t$) into a single, eternal frequency state ($\omega$). It suggests that "Enlightenment" or "Flow" is the mathematical operation of converting the history of the self into the vibration of the present.

## 4. The Uncertainty Principle of Identity

Applying Fourier analysis to these domains yields a cognitive analogue to the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle:

$$
\Delta \text{Identity} \cdot \Delta \text{Resonance} \ge \frac{\mathbb{C}}{2}
$$

This inequality dictates a fundamental trade-off: one cannot simultaneously maximize the precision of discrete identity (separation) and universal resonance (connection). To maximize connection to the whole (High Resonance), the definition of the specific self (Identity) must become less rigid.
